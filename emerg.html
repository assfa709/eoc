Cholera Outbreak

So far 1297 cases of cholera have been reported in the region since the outbreak was confirmed in Hawassa city on 27 April 2023 with a total death of 12 (CFR=0.93). No new cases have been reported in more than two months from any woreda. All CTCs/CTUs have been closed. Preventions activities are still ongoing.

Measles outbreak

As of 3 Feb. 2024, 1163 cases of measles have been reported since the outbreak has been declared in the region at the beginning of May 2023. The outbreak has affected 26 woredas so far. The number of cases has increased by 162 (16%) in the week. A total of 13 related deaths have been reported including the one reported in this week. The outbreak is rapidly spreading throughout the region especially in recent weeks. Mop-up vaccinations have been conducted in the outbreak woredas and patients with complications have been treated in HFs.

Malaria

Considering epi-week 04 of 2024 report, a total of 14178 suspected cases of malaria were tested either by RDT or microscope at different health facilities of the region. Out of the tested, 2735 (19%) were found positive for malaria. The number of malaria cases has decreased by 298 cases (10%) with increase of lab tests while positivity rate decreased compared to the previous week. While this number of malaria cases is very high compared to the threshold line, it has a decrease of 13% compared to the number of cases of similar week of 2023. In this week 21 cases were reported from inpatient. Hawassa city reported most of the cases (1058) followed by Hawassa Zuria (222), and Loka Abaya (200). With respect to test positivity rate, Bensa woreda leads by 53% followed by Chabe (51%), and Chirone (50%). Considering by attack rate (AR) per 100,000 population, Wondo Genet town (375), Hawassa City (253) and Loka Abaya (171) hold the top three places. No malaria related death was reported in the week.

Malnutrition

During this reporting epi-week 04 of 2024, a total of 310 severe acute malnutrition cases and 676 moderate acute malnutrition cases of under- five children have been reported. SAM cases decreased by 2 cases in the week compared to the previous week while MAM increased by 148 cases (28%) among under five children. A total of 703 moderate acute malnutrition cases of pregnant and lactating women were reported in the week, increasing by 127 cases or 22% from the previous week. From the 310 SAM cases of under five children, 78 (25%) were reported from SC sites while the remaining were from OTP. No malnutrition related death has been reported in the week. The top three woredas/towns contributing to the reported SAM cases of under-five children in the region were Hawassa city (44, 14%), Hawassa Zuria (30, 10%), and Dale (28, 9%). On the other hand, Dara (168), Hawassa Zuria (150), and Aleta Wondo (69) woredas reported the highest MAM cases of under-5 children. Considering MAM case among pregnant and lactating women, Hawassa Zuria (130), Dara (124), and Derara (77) reported the highest cases.
